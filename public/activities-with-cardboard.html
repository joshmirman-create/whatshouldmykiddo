Why cardboard is such a great material

Cardboard is easy to cut, tape, draw on, and paint. It's rigid enough to build with but forgiving enough that mistakes don't ruin the project. And it's free — something most households have in abundance. The only limit is imagination and the willingness to let kids make a mess of it.

Cardboard activities by age

  • 2-3 years — painting boxes, turning boxes into cars or houses for dolls, simple stacking
  • 4-5 years — building vehicles, making puppet theaters, cardboard animals, simple mazes
  • 6-8 years — marble runs, elaborate cities, engineering challenges, model buildings
  • 9+ years — complex mechanisms, architectural models, stop-motion sets, game boards

Cardboard building tips

Masking tape works better than scotch tape for cardboard construction — it sticks more securely and can be painted over. Cutting cardboard is easier with a box cutter (adult only) than scissors, which tend to bend and crush cardboard edges.

Frequently asked questions

What can kids make with cardboard boxes?

Playhouses, cars, spaceships, cities, marble runs, puppet theaters, mazes, robots, dollhouses, and endless other creations. Cardboard is one of the most versatile craft materials available and it's free.

What do you do with cardboard boxes for toddlers?

At toddler age, large boxes work well as playhouses, cars to sit in, or painting canvases. Smaller boxes can be stacked, sorted, and arranged. The activity doesn't need to be elaborate — toddlers find open-ended box play genuinely engaging.
