Activities kids actually want to do

Every activity on this site has been tested with real kids. Full steps, parent tips, and variations so you can adjust on the fly.

🎨

Color Scavenger Hunt

Pick a color, set a timer, collect everything that color in the house. Invented by a real 4-year-old. Works for ages 2 and up, takes 15 minutes, zero mess.

🏔️

Mt. Mom and Dad

Parents lie on the floor. Kids climb them. Win by getting three stickers on a forehead. Perfect for tired parents who need to lie down anyway.

🎭

Shadow Theater Night

Turn off the lights. Hand them a flashlight. Give them a premise — a hero whose powers are fading, two characters from different worlds meeting for the first time. They improvise the whole show.

📦

Number Box

Write 0 to 9 on a cardboard box. Big numbers are easy targets, small numbers are hard. Throw socks at it for points. First to 50 wins. Math disguised as a sport.

Why parents use this instead of searching

Most activity searches return the same ten ideas — fort building, baking soda volcanoes, finger painting. Generic suggestions that might work for any child, which means they're perfectly designed for no child in particular.

This site asks three questions: how old is your kid, what are they obsessed with right now, and what do you actually have at home. The answer it gives back is built around those three things specifically. A 5-year-old who loves shapes and has magnet tiles gets something completely different from a 5-year-old who loves dinosaurs and has cardboard boxes.

The activity comes with full steps, a parent tip, and variations for making it easier, harder, quieter, or more active depending on how the afternoon is going. No shopping required. No prep beyond reading the steps once.

For the moments that sneak up on you

Rainy Saturday mornings. The hour after school before dinner. A sick day when screens have already run out of mileage. A long car ride with nothing packed. These are the moments parents actually need help with — not Pinterest-perfect craft projects that require a trip to the store.

Every activity on this site uses materials most families already have: paper, crayons, tape, cardboard boxes, socks, a flashlight. The generator never suggests something that requires buying anything. If it calls for materials, they're on your list of what you said you have at home.

What do I need to use this?

Just tell us your child's age, what they're into right now, and what materials you have at home. We build a personalized activity using only what you already have — no shopping required.

How long does it take to get an activity?

A few minutes to get your activity. Most activities keep kids engaged for 20 to 60 minutes. The 15-minute collection has activities you can start in under a minute.

What ages does this work for?

Infants through teens. The generator tailors suggestions to the specific age so a 2-year-old and a 10-year-old get completely different activities. Age-appropriate difficulty, materials, and complexity are all factored in.

Is this free?

Yes, completely free. We suggest optional products and books that can enhance activities, but every activity works with what you already have at home. No account required.

What if I don't like the activity it generates?

Hit New Activity and try again. You can also tweak your answers — change the energy level, swap out a material, adjust the age — and get something different. The generator has enough variety that two runs with the same inputs rarely produce the same result.

Can I save activities for later?

Yes. Every activity page has a Save button that stores it on your device. Your saved activities live at whatshouldmykiddo.com/saved and are available any time — no account, no login, no data collected.

Do you collect data about my child?

No. This site is COPPA compliant. Parents fill out the activity builder — children never interact with the site directly. No children's data is collected, stored, or shared.
